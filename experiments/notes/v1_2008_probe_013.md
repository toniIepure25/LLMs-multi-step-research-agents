## Focused Assessment

### Did the sharper OTC-derivatives claim return or stay preserved?

Yes.

`013` kept:

- `Deregulation of the OTC derivatives market contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

So the sharper same-event mechanism survived again after the candidate-generation
hardening.

### Did the over-specific securitization phrasing stay gone?

Partially yes.

The bad full-set claim from `v1_tier1_eval_set_001`:

- `Securitization of subprime mortgages contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

did not return.

But the replacement securitization claim in `013` is still more evidence-shaped
and verbose than ideal:

- `Securitization of loans into securities that are then sold to investors contributed...`

So the unsupported over-specific phrasing is gone, but the claim is not as
clean as `012` or `006`.

### Did groundedness recover or remain strong?

Yes.

`013` kept `groundedness=1.0`, matching `012` and `006`, and recovering fully
from the weak `v1_tier1_eval_set_001` 2008 run.

### Did claim count remain healthy?

Yes.

`013` kept `3` claims, improving over the `2`-claim collapse in the full-set
`v1_tier1_eval_set_001` 2008 run.

### Did candidate coverage appear healthier?

Yes.

This is the clearest improvement over the weak full-set 2008 run:

- the full-set run only kept 2 claims and missed the sharper OTC mechanism
- `013` recovered 3 supported claims
- the claim set now covers:
  - securitization
  - OTC-derivatives deregulation
  - subprime lending

That is a healthier mechanism mix than the failed `v1_tier1_eval_set_001`
output.

### Did direct causal phrasing remain clean?

Mostly.

All 3 claims are still direct causal answers and remain on the exact goal
event. The one caveat is the securitization claim, which is more copied and
definition-shaped than the cleaner phrasing in `012` and `006`.

Mostly yes.
