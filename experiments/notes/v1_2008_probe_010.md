## Focused Assessment

### Did the `housing crisis` drift disappear?

Yes.

The alternate-target claim from `009`:

- `The securitization of subprime mortgages contributed to the housing crisis.`

did not survive in `010`.

### Did the sharper same-event mechanism claim survive?

Yes.

The broader deregulation claim from `009`:

- `Deregulation of the financial system contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

was replaced by the sharper:

- `Deregulation of the OTC derivatives market contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

This is a real improvement in selector behavior on the exact weakness we were
targeting.

### Did direct causal phrasing remain clean?

Yes.

All 3 `010` claims are short direct-causal answers, and the snippet-like
phrasing from `004` stayed gone.

### Did groundedness remain acceptable?

Only partially.

`010` regressed from `groundedness=1.0` in `004`, `006`, and `009` to
`0.6667`.

The issue was the securitization claim:

- `Securitization contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

It was supported by only one securitization evidence item and verification
marked it `insufficient` because lexical support was too weak.

### Did claim count remain healthy?

Yes.

`010` kept `3` claims, so the selector did not collapse the answer set.

### Did distinct supported mechanisms improve?

Partially.

`010` is better than `009` because it now preserves:

- securitization
- a sharper OTC-derivatives deregulation claim
- a separate regulation/supervision failures claim

This is more diverse and more on-target than `009`.

But it is still not clearly better than `006`, because `006` kept all 3 claims
supported and `010` did not.

## Interpretation

This probe shows a real selector win and a real support-quality regression.

Real improvement:

- no alternate-target housing-crisis drift
- sharper OTC-derivatives mechanism restored
- broad same-family deregulation wording no longer dominates
- direct causal phrasing stayed clean

Regression:

- groundedness dropped to `0.6667`
- evidence utilization dropped from `0.625` in `009` to `0.4286`
- securitization support weakened enough to become `insufficient`

So the new selector hardening resolved the exact ranking failure we were
targeting, but the overall live result is still not stable enough to beat the
best prior 2008 probe.

## Overall Judgment

### Did the selector fix resolve the target-event fidelity and mechanism-preservation issues?

Yes, on those narrow issues.

- the `housing crisis` drift disappeared
- the sharper OTC-derivatives claim survived
- the broader same-family umbrella claim was effectively down-ranked

### Is v1-minimal now strong enough to justify a full 3-question live rerun next?

No.

The selector changes fixed the intended ranking problem, but `010` introduced a
new support-quality regression. One more narrow v1-minimal pass is the safer
next step before rerunning the full Tier 1 set.
