## Focused Assessment Of The Targeted Synthesis Weakness

### Did the 2008 claims become more directly causal?

Yes.

The two copied/descriptive securitization claims in `004` were replaced by one short direct causal claim in `006`:

- `004`: descriptive snippet-like securitization wording
- `006`: `Securitization contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

This is a real improvement in answer shape.

### Did copied/snippet-like phrasing decrease?

Yes, clearly.

The specific bad pattern from `004`:
- `is more than just a capital markets innovation...`
- `provides an additional funding source...`

did not survive in `006`.

### Did groundedness remain acceptable?

Yes.

- `groundedness` stayed at `1.0`
- all 3 claims remained `supported`

## New Caution / Residual Noise

The new third claim is cleaner in shape, but still not ideal:

- `The failure of monetary policy contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

This is on-question, but it appears to be synthesized from a broad timeline/history snippet rather than from especially crisp causal evidence. So the hardening improved phrasing, but it did not eliminate all synthesis variance.

This looks like residual provider/search noise plus weak evidence semantics, not a regression in the retrieval fix.

## Overall Judgment

### Did the targeted synthesis-quality issue improve?

Yes.

The exact weakness we were targeting in the 2008 run improved:
- claims are more directly causal
- copied/snippet-like phrasing decreased substantially
- groundedness did not regress

### Were there regressions?

No major regression.

The main caveat is that the monetary-policy claim is still weaker than ideal, but it is still on-question and grounded enough for v0.

### Is this fix strong enough to justify rerunning the full 3-question set?

Yes.

The weakest known run improved on the exact quality issue we were targeting, without harming groundedness or evidence discipline.
