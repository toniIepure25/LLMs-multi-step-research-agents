Yes.

### Did direct causal phrasing remain clean?

Mostly yes.

Not fully.

The broader claim:

returned alongside the sharper:

- `The deregulated OTC derivatives market contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

So the support-quality fix worked, but same-family mechanism pruning is still
not fully stable in live conditions.

## Interpretation

`011` resolves the exact support-quality regression from `010`.

Real improvements versus `010`:

- groundedness recovered from `0.6667` to `1.0`
- verification recovered from `supported=2, insufficient=1` to `supported=3`
- the weak securitization-support issue disappeared
- the housing-crisis drift stayed gone
- the sharper OTC-derivatives claim stayed preserved

What is still not ideal:

- the broader deregulation umbrella claim returned alongside the sharper OTC claim
- evidence utilization dropped to `0.3333`, below `010`, `009`, and `006`
- mechanism diversity is still not clearly better than `006`

So this is a real recovery on the targeted support-quality weakness, but not a
fully stable final 2008 result yet.

## Overall Judgment

### Did the support-preserving selector fix resolve the targeted tradeoff?

Yes.

The weak securitization support problem that broke `010` did not repeat in
`011`.

### Is `011` clearly better than `010`?

Yes.

`011` is better on the exact tradeoff we were targeting:

- groundedness recovered
- all claims are supported
- the housing-crisis drift stayed gone
- the OTC claim stayed preserved

### Is `011` clearly better than the best prior 2008 run?

Not clearly.

It is competitive with `006`, but not an obvious upgrade because the broader
same-family deregulation claim returned and evidence utilization is weaker.

### Is v1-minimal now strong enough to justify a full 3-question live rerun next?

Not yet.

One more narrow v1-minimal pass is still the safer next step, focused on
preventing a broader same-family umbrella claim from surviving alongside a
sharper supported mechanism claim in the same run.
