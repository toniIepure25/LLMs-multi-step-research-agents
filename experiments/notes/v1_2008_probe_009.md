## Focused Assessment

### Did the selector help preserve better-supported direct causal claims?

Yes, partially.

Compared with `007` and `008`, `009` clearly recovered the good properties that
had been lost:

- claim count recovered from `2` to `3`
- groundedness recovered from `0.5` to `1.0`
- all 3 claims were verified as `supported`
- direct-causal claim shape stayed clean

So the selector appears to have helped preserve supported direct-causal claims
instead of over-filtering the result set.

### Did the 2008 claims stay directly causal?

Mostly yes.

`009` avoided the old snippet-like `004` wording and kept short causal
sentences:

- `Securitization contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`
- `Deregulation of the financial system contributed to the 2008 financial crisis.`

The third claim is also causal in shape, but it drifts to the housing crisis
instead of answering the user’s exact target event:

- `The securitization of subprime mortgages contributed to the housing crisis.`

### Did distinct supported mechanisms remain preserved?

Only partially.

This run is better than `007` and `008` because it preserved 3 supported claims,
but it is still weaker than `006` on mechanism diversity.

`006` preserved 3 cleaner distinct mechanisms:

- securitization
- OTC derivatives deregulation
- monetary policy

`009` preserved:

- securitization
- broad financial deregulation
- a second securitization-adjacent housing-crisis claim

So the selector recovered support quality and claim count, but it did not yet
consistently preserve the strongest distinct mechanism set.

### Did vague umbrella or snippet-like phrasing stay reduced?

Yes.

- the snippet-like securitization wording from `004` remained gone
- vague umbrella phrasing did not reappear

### Did groundedness remain acceptable?

Yes.

`009` recovered to `groundedness=1.0`, matching `004` and `006` and clearly
improving on `007` and `008`.

### Did claim count remain healthy?

Yes.

`009` returned to `3` claims, which is consistent with the healthier prior runs
and avoids the collapsed `2`-claim pattern from `007` and `008`.

## Interpretation

This is a real v1-minimal improvement over the weak late-stage v0 probes.

What improved:

- direct causal phrasing stayed clean
- groundedness recovered to `1.0`
- claim count recovered to `3`
- evidence utilization improved to `0.625`, the strongest of the compared 2008 runs

What is still not ideal:

- the selector did not preserve the best distinct mechanism mix from `006`
- one final claim drifted from the 2008 financial crisis to the housing crisis
- the deregulation claim is broader than the cleaner OTC-derivatives claim in `006`

So `009` is stronger than `007` and `008`, and materially better than the `004`
baseline on phrasing quality, but it is not an unambiguous win over `006`.

## Overall Judgment

### Did v1-minimal improve the 2008 case in live conditions?

Yes, with a caveat.

It improved the unstable late-stage failure mode:

- support quality recovered
- claim count recovered
- direct-causal phrasing stayed clean

But the selector still needs a little more precision on target-event fidelity
and mechanism diversity before this can be called a fully stable upgrade over
the best prior focused run.

### Is v1-minimal strong enough to justify a full 3-question live rerun next?

Not yet.

This is a promising first live checkpoint, but the housing-crisis drift in one
selected claim is a narrow remaining issue. One more focused v1-minimal pass is
the safer next step before rerunning the full 3-question Tier 1 set.
